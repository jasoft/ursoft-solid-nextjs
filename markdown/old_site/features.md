What's troubling us?
--------------------

I t's not uncommon to come across stubborn programs that refuse to uninstall completely, leaving behind remnants or "artifacts" on your system registry and hard drive. These remnants can weigh your computer down, resulting in sluggish performance, unexpected errors, and frequent crashes.

Imagine installing trial versions of programs to browse through and test out. But, at the end of the day, you decide some of the programs are not to your liking and delete them. However, some programs are difficult to uninstall entirely, and they linger on your Windows installed programs list even when they are thought to be gone. The presence of these improperly uninstalled programs can cause errors, as junk files, and registry keys/values are left behind. These "junk artifacts" become problematic over time, slowing down Windows performance substantially.
